Karin Waidley, PhD
Who i am
I am Karin Waidley, a passionate and versatile professional deeply immersed in the world of theatre, education, and scholarly pursuits. Rooted in a commitment to using the transformative power of theatre for social change, I've dedicated my career to fostering understanding and addressing critical issues, particularly in the realm of violence prevention. With a foundation in both performance and academia, I bring a unique perspective to my work, seeking to bridge the gap between theory and practice.
What i do
In the dynamic intersection of teaching, artistry, and scholarship, I craft narratives and experiences that resonate on both personal and societal levels. My professional journey encompasses founding and directing impactful theatre programs, both locally and internationally, and spearheading initiatives that harness the potential of theatre for social justice. As a Resident Dramaturg at the University of Michigan and through collaborative projects with Kenyan artists, I continue to direct, dramaturg, and engage in research that explores the intricate connections between storytelling, embodied performance, and the neurobiology of trauma.
My Qualifications
Armed with a Masters in Acting from Northwestern and a PhD in Theatre from the University of Washington, my academic journey has been marked by a deep commitment to rigorous scholarship and practical application. I've been honoured with a Fulbright Scholarship and Specialist designation, extending my impact globally. With a wealth of experience as a Professor and Director of the Theatre program at Western Colorado University, I bring a rich blend of theoretical knowledge and practical expertise to my roles.
My Values
At the core of my professional ethos lies a commitment to using theatre as a catalyst for positive change. My values are rooted in social justice, education, and community engagement. I believe in the power of storytelling and embodied performance to not only entertain but also to create empathetic understanding and address societal challenges. Collaborative, innovative, and socially conscious, I strive to infuse my work with these values, whether directing a play, leading educational initiatives, or contributing to scholarly publications.
