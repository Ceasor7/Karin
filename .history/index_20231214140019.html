I'm Karin Waidley, a versatile professional encompassing roles as an educator, artist, and scholar. Armed with a Masters in Acting and a PhD in Theatre, my academic journey has centred on harnessing the power of theatre for violence prevention. I founded a programme for adjudicated youth and directed the Theatre program at Western Colorado University, specializing in original works and theatre for social justice. As a Fulbright Scholar and current Resident Dramaturg at the University of Michigan, I've made a global impact, addressing gender-based violence in Wyoming and collaborating with Kenyan artists on initiatives like Theatre for Development. My ongoing research explores the nexus of storytelling, embodied performance, and the neurobiology of trauma. A published author, I'm also editing an anthology on Kenyan Theatre History and serve as the Editor of Theatre/Practice journal.
